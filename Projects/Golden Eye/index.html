Real-Time Satellite Tracker

Start Azimuth: The compass direction where the satellite will first appear in the sky, measured in degrees from true north. For example, it could be 45° (northeast).

Max Elevation: The highest point the satellite will reach in the sky during its pass over your location, measured in degrees above the horizon. A max elevation of 90° means the satellite will pass directly overhead.

End Azimuth: The direction where the satellite will disappear from view as it moves across the sky, measured in degrees from true north.

Total Duration: The total time the satellite will be visible from the moment it first appears to when it disappears.

This data gives you precise information about when and where to observe the satellite pass over your location, including the direction, height, and duration of the pass. The location data feed of the International Space Station is selected here for demonstration purposes.
